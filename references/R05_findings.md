## L09 — Reproduction-figure honesty

Three reproduction figures live in the simulator README:
`reproduce_stocker_simoncelli_fig4.png`,
`reproduce_wei_stocker_fig4.png`, `reproduce_acerbi_fig1.png`. They
are generated by `tests/make_paper_figures.m` (per R1 L02 audit).
The claim attached to them is "the simulator recovers the published
signatures in each theoretical family."

Honesty audit — three distinct failure modes a reader may not see:

1. **Parametric re-instantiation ≠ faithful re-implementation.**
   Wei-Stocker 2015 fit the orientation model to Girshick et al.
   2011 natural-image prior; the simulator's `reproduce_wei_stocker_fig4.png`
   uses a *generic* skew-normal / Gaussian prior (see Wei-Stocker 2015
   Fig. 4 uses $p(\theta) \propto 2 - |\sin\theta|$). If the
   reproduction plot uses an idealised prior rather than the
   Girshick one, the signature may match but the empirical content
   is weaker than the caption implies.
2. **Parameter-sweep vs. published fit.** Stocker-Simoncelli 2006
   report per-subject likelihood widths that vary across contrasts;
   the simulator's `reproduce_stocker_simoncelli_fig4.png` may use
   a single chosen contrast rather than the full grid. This is
   fine for "simulator reproduces the shape" but not for
   "simulator matches the published data."
3. **Data-point match vs. model-prediction match.** The proper
   reproduction claim is: simulator's BLS observer with the
   published (prior, likelihood) produces the published mean bias
   curve. If the figure shows model curves *overlaid on simulated
   data* without overlaying published data, the reader cannot
   check the match.

**Refs outside 8-anchor (L09)**: Hedges et al. 2011 (internal
critique of Stocker-Simoncelli identifiability), Sotiropoulos et al.
2014 (revisited speed prior), Jogan & Stocker 2015 (improved
reverse-engineering). **3 widening citations here.**

## L18 — Replication status of 8 anchor papers

Survey of independent replications / challenges / supersessions:

| Anchor | Status | Representative independent work |
|---|---|---|
| Stocker-Simoncelli 2006 | Replicated but identifiability critiqued | Hedges et al. 2011; Sotiropoulos et al. 2014; Jogan-Stocker 2015; Zhang-Stocker 2021 refines the method |
| Jazayeri-Shadlen 2010 | Robustly replicated across labs | Remington, Narain, Jazayeri 2018 (cerebellar timing); Wang, Narain et al. 2018 (temporal scaling); Ren et al. 2022 TICS review |
| Acerbi 2012 | Replicated in timing | Wang-Luo-Ivry 2023 (unitary adaptation); Bévalot-Meyniel 2023 (identifiability verification); Sohn-Lee 2013 (prior-width learning) |
| Wei-Stocker 2015 | Replicated + extended | Taylor & Bays 2018 (orientation); Noel et al. 2021 (categorical decoding + efficient coding); Fritsche-Spaak-De Lange 2020 (dual-prior observer) |
| Wei-Stocker 2016 | Theoretical; no direct replication | Park-Pillow 2024 Bayesian Efficient Coding extends |
| Hahn-Wei 2022 / 2024 | Recent; sparse independent replications | Only the authors' own further work; <2 years since publication |
| Hahn-Wang-Wei 2025 | Preprint; no replications | — |
| Prat-Carrabin-Gershman 2025 | Recent; minimal independent replication | Prat-Carrabin-Woodford 2020 precursor |

### Patterns
1. The classical anchors (Stocker-Simoncelli 2006, Jazayeri-Shadlen
   2010, Acerbi 2012, Wei-Stocker 2015) each have ≥ 3 independent
   follow-ups or methodological extensions. The theoretical
   framework holds, but details (specific fitted priors; exact
   encoding parameters) are contested.
2. The identifiability concern is internal to the Wei-Stocker
   tradition: Zhang-Stocker 2021 explicitly states "reverse-
   engineered priors in previous studies showed large variations
   across subjects, indicating a potential case of overfitting due
   to insufficient model constraints (Stocker & Simoncelli 2006;
   Hedges et al. 2011; Sotiropoulos et al. 2014; Jogan & Stocker
   2015)." This is *the* field-internal critique the simulator
   should inherit. HWW 2025 is the formal response to this
   concern.
3. The recent anchors (HW 2022/2024, HWW 2025, PCG 2025) are
   all < 3 years old; replication ecosystem has not caught up.
   The simulator cites them as foundational but treats them as
   if they were textbook-established. They are not.

### Specific challenges to the 8 anchors

- **Stocker-Simoncelli 2006 internal critique**: Hedges et al. 2011
  reported that the reverse-engineered priors were sensitive to
  the choice of likelihood function form.
- **Wei-Stocker 2015 challenge**: Prat-Carrabin-Gershman 2025
  explicitly argues that Weber behaviour does not require efficient
  coding — a challenge to the claim that repulsive biases are
  uniquely predicted by efficient-CDF encoding.
- **Hahn-Wang-Wei 2025 caveat**: the formal identifiability
  guarantee requires multi-noise-level data (R2 L07); practical
  transfer to timing is partial (R2 accepted this).

## L15 — Confounders in mirror-skew design (secondary)

Serial dependence is the dominant within-task confounder in duration
reproduction. The literature converges on several concrete
mechanisms:

- **Sensory carryover** (Cicchini-Mikellidou-Burr 2018): bias from
  prior stimulus value.
- **Decisional carryover** (Pilly-Seitz 2019; Li-Wang-Zaidel 2026):
  bias from prior response, often in opposite direction from
  sensory carryover.
- **Task-switching amplification** (Cheng-Chen-Yang-Shi 2024):
  sequential effect enhanced in reproduction-after-reproduction
  pairs, less in reproduction-after-bisection.
- **Motor-response coupling** (Wu et al. 2024): serial dependence
  in timing requires consistent motor responses.
- **Timing of the effect** (Bliss-Sun-D'Esposito 2017;
  Pascucci et al. 2023): serial dependence absent at perception,
  emerges in working-memory retention; therefore post-perceptual.
- **Meta-analysis** (Manassi-Murai-Whitney 2023): serial dependence
  is robust across stimulus classes, modalities, and labs.

### Implication for mirror-skew

If the experimenter randomly interleaves A and R trials, serial
dependence from the previous trial's prior class contaminates the
current trial's bias estimate — the (b_A, b_R) sign pattern will
be attenuated by an amount ∝ the effect size of sequential bias.
If trials are blocked by condition, within-block serial dependence
persists but does NOT cross-contaminate — sign pattern is
preserved at the cost of within-subject averaging.

The simulator currently assumes iid trials with no serial
dependence. This is a violated assumption. The $\alpha_{\mathrm{sda}}$
estimates will inherit a serial-dependence contribution that
confirmatory analysis must separate.
